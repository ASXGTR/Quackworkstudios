Welcome to Duckmon: Collectable Card Multiverse

Duckmon began as a small minigame inside my TextZ Ombie project, but it quickly grew into something much bigger. Inspired by Triple Triad (Final Fantasy VIII) and classic collectable, Pokémon style card collecting, Duckmon blends strategic grid based battles with a multiverse of unique duck factions, lore, and rarity systems.

About the Game

Duckmon is a multiverse‑driven collectible card battler built around fast, tactical, grid‑based combat. What began as a tiny minigame inside an old prototype has evolved into a full standalone game with deep strategy, unique factions, and an expanding universe of Duckmon variants. Every card represents a creature, anomaly, or corrupted form pulled from a fractured reality — each with its own stats, abilities, and identity.

Duckmon now includes a full Story Mode spanning 30 stories across 10 chapters, each with unique art, choices, branching routes, and multiple endings. The upcoming demo features one complete story from Chapter 1, including 10 endings, hidden paths, and many wonderfully avoidable death scenes.

The game is being built openly, iteratively, and with a lot of stubborn solo‑dev energy. The multiverse expands one card, one faction, and one story at a time.

Core Features

• Tactical 5×4 Grid Battles
Solo skirmish mode with three difficulties — Easy, Normal, and Hard — built around clean, strategic card placement and tile control.

• 30-Story, 10-Chapter Story Mode (In Development)
A full narrative campaign exploring the Duckmon multiverse. The demo includes one complete Chapter 1 story with 10 endings and multiple death scenes.

• 310 Fully Illustrated Cards
Each card has unique stats, rarity, and faction identity across 10 main factions and the rare Mythic hybrids.

• Full Deckbuilder & Binder
Build, save, and load multiple decks. Browse your full collection with filters, rarity sorting, and faction views.

• Progression & Rewards
Packs, currency, achievements, login rewards, player profiles, and persistent cloud saves powered by Supabase.

• Clean, Fair Collection System
No gacha, no pay-to-win. Cards are earned through gameplay, story mode, and progression.

• Built in Unity by a Solo Developer
Duckmon grows one card, one faction, and one story at a time.

Gameplay

Battles take place on a 5×4 grid in turn-based strategic combat. Each player builds a 10 card deck and uses positioning, faction bonuses, and card abilities to outplay their opponent.

Progression & Rewards

Cards are obtained through packs, currency, events, and promotions. Players level up, earn rewards, unlock achievements, and climb leaderboards.

Roadmap

Duckmon is being built openly and iteratively. This roadmap outlines the current development focus, upcoming demo plans, and long-term goals for expanding the game’s systems, story mode, and future game modes.

Stage 1 — Active Development (Current)

• Finalizing story art for Chapter 1
• Implementing the first demo story block (10 endings + death scenes)
• Debugging active systems and refining core gameplay
• Rewriting and formatting backend code for server-authoritative structure
• QA testing across skirmish mode and story mode
• Audio adjustments and soundtrack balancing
• Finalizing pack creation, rarity tables, and card data
• Playtesting skirmish mode across Easy / Normal / Hard difficulties

Stage 2 — Demo v1.0 Release (Coming Soon)

• Preparing the first public demo for itch.io (Windows ZIP build)
• Final polish pass for UI, UX, and story presentation
• Packaging the demo for distribution
• Estimated release window: ~1 month
• Demo includes: 1 full story, 10 endings, unique art, and skirmish mode

Stage 3 — Post-Demo Content Expansion

• Continuing story art production for Chapters 2–10
• Writing and implementing new stories and branching paths
• Expanding rewards and progression systems
• Creating a Story Art Gallery for unlocked illustrations
• Adding an in-game Audio Player for soundtrack unlocks
• Listening to player feedback and adjusting systems accordingly

Stage 4 — Gameplay Expansion & Systems Upgrade

• Implementing faction-specific battle grids with unique bonuses
• Adding buffs, debuffs, tile effects, and chain reaction mechanics
• Expanding solo game modes with new challenges and variants
• Continued QA testing, debugging, and performance improvements
• Integrating community feedback into balance and design

Stage 5 — Demo v2.0 Release

• Releasing Demo v2.0 with expanded features and new content
• Updated story chapters, new cards, and improved UI/UX
• Gathering player feedback for the next development cycle

Stage 6 — Future Game Modes (Exploration)

• Team Battles (10v10) — Pokémon-style team combat with abilities and movesets
• Tower Defense Mode — Linear-path defense using cards as towers
• Adding simple ability effects, animations, and combat systems
• Expanding faction identity through unique mechanics

Stage 7 — Online Features & Steam Preparation

• Leaderboards and competitive ranking
• PvP prototype and backend networking
• Friends system (Steam integration planned)
• Preparing Demo v3.0 for Steam release
• Final polish, bug fixing, and long-term support planning
