Welcome to Duckmon: Collectable Card Multiverse

Duckmon began as a small minigame inside my TextZ Ombie project, but it quickly grew into something much bigger. Inspired by Triple Triad (Final Fantasy VIII) and classic collectable, Pokémon style card collecting, Duckmon blends strategic grid based battles with a multiverse of unique duck factions, lore, and rarity systems.

About the Game

Duckmon is a feature-rich collectible card game built around strategic grid-based battles, deck construction, and long-term progression. The core systems are fully implemented, including deck building, card packs, achievements, rewards, player profiles, and persistent cloud data. Current development is focused on PvE AI, balance tuning, UI refinement, and playtesting.

Core Features

• Multi-deck builder with save/load persistence
• Pack opening animations with validated RNG tables
• Achievement system with reward triggers
• Player profiles, avatars, and unlockables
• Full card binder integrated with deck building
• Market and economy systems
• Daily login rewards and progression
• Backend persistence powered by Supabase

Duckmon includes 310 fully illustrated cards, each with unique stats, lore, and rarity. The 10 main factions are: Electric, Fire, Holy, Ice, Nature, Physical, Technology, Toxic, Undead, and Wind. The 11th faction, Mythic, contains 10 dual-faction cards that blend elemental traits with Mythic power.

Gameplay

Battles take place on a 5×4 grid in turn-based strategic combat. Each player builds a 10-card deck and uses positioning, faction bonuses, and card abilities to outplay their opponent. Duckmon includes both PvE and PvP modes, with ranked leaderboards and seasonal rewards.

Progression & Rewards

Cards are obtained through packs, in-game currency, events, and special promotions. Players level up, earn experience, unlock rewards, and receive daily login bonuses. Achievements and leaderboards give long-term goals and bragging rights.
