About Me

Solo dev, dad, husband, building games one stubborn idea at a time.

Say hi

My name is John, late 30s, solo game dev, dad, husband, and the creator of Quackwork Studios. What started as a hobby quickly turned into a full creative obsession. I build in Unity, experiment with AI tools, and learn whatever I need to bring the ideas in my head into reality, from C# to GitHub workflows to the HTML that powers this site.

My first real project was TextZ Ombie, a 2D survival horror, choose your own adventure hybrid filled with systems, inventory, stats, diseases, choices, and multiple playable characters. I worked on it for six months across two attempts. It did not ship, and it now lives in my archive, but it taught me everything, systems thinking, UI, pacing, writing, and how deep the rabbit hole goes when you try to build something bigger than yourself. It humbled me, and it shaped me.

Then came Duckmon. It started as a tiny collectible card minigame inside TextZ Ombie, but it quickly became too fun, too chaotic, and too full of potential to stay small. It evolved into its own standalone universe, a lore heavy, multiverse spanning 2D card battler inspired by Triple Triad. Ducks replaced zombies, and suddenly everything clicked. Duckmon is now my main project and the heart of Quackwork Studios.

I focus mainly on 2D Unity development, and I handle everything myself, art, gameplay, systems, coding, audio, design, direction, and all the weird glue in between. I openly use AI tools, not as shortcuts, but as creative amplifiers. Every mechanic, every asset, every idea still comes through me. I am the director, the AI is just part of the toolkit.
